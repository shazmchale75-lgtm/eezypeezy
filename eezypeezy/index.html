The oldest idea in cooking

Europe's greatest dishes
started with almost nothing.

Long before recipe apps, meal kits and supermarket sauces, generations of home cooks across Europe fed their families beautifully from a handful of simple things. They didn't think of it as cooking with odds and ends. They just called it dinner. The Italians even gave this wisdom a name — cucina povera — "the kitchen of the poor." Today, these dishes are served in Michelin-starred restaurants. Nothing has changed except what we believe we're capable of.

🇮🇹
Spaghetti Aglio e Olio
Italy
Just pasta, garlic, olive oil, chilli flakes. Nothing more. Beloved for centuries.
🇪🇸
Tortilla Española
Spain
Just eggs, potatoes, olive oil, and an onion. One of Spain's most iconic dishes.
🇫🇷
Soupe au Pistou
France
Just whatever vegetables were in the garden, with a handful of basil and garlic.
🇮🇪
Irish Stew
Ireland
Just meat, potatoes, carrots, and an onion. Fed generations of Irish families.
"Your grandparents didn't have a jar of sauce or a TV chef.
They had what they had — and they fed a family.
You have more than they did."
